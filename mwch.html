MUIR WOODS COFFEE HOUSE

WINTER 2015 | Web Developer & UX Researcher


UC San Diego's on-campus coffee vendor Muir Woods Coffee House was in need of their own website to help promote themselves to both students and non-students. Their current mode of promotion was only by word of mouth and through an extension website from UCSD. So in a team of 3, we worked closely with our client, the Director of Muir Woods Coffee House, in order to create a website that met his needs.

Type: Web Development, Graphic Design, Web Design, Branding

CORE RESPONSIBLITIES
  • Aided in user/client interviews, creating user personae, and competitive analysis
  • Executed a majority of the front-end coding via HTML/SCSS
  • Creating the graphics for the menu page
  • Took and edited all the photos on the website (with the exception of the Muristas photos)
  • Tools Used: Adobe Photoshop CS5, Axure, HTML/CSS, SASS, Javascript


USER RESEARCH

We interviewed the Director of Muir Woods Coffee House to find out what his needs were for the website. We also interviewed around ~20 users to find out what their needs were from a coffee website.


Client Needs User Needs
  • Earthy color scheme/Chalkboard theme that matches with the vibe of Muir Woods
  • Stories/Testimonials from past customers/employees
  • Less emphasis on retail-side of the shop
  • History and Mission of Muir Woods Coffee House
  • "Muirista" presenting the team
  • Listing of where they source their coffee beans, loose-leaf tea, and pastries from
  • Menu with prices clearly marked
  • Location and hours shown prominently
  • Aesthetically pleasing images of the coffee and environment
  • Reviews and ratings
  • Indication if there’s free wifi and outlets available
  • Know how crowded it is at a certain time
  • Comfortable, friendly vibe from the website that’s representative of the shop

PROTOTYPES LEARNINGS RECAP

After working with a real client, it was interesting to see how different client needs are from the target user’s needs. Before this project, I didn’t realize that these needs could and would clash so I wasn’t exactly sure how to balance them once they did clash. It’s difficult to decide whether we should listen to the client because they’d be the one paying for our services or listen to the end users since they’ll be the ones using and determining the success of the product. But as we worked through the project, we all learned how to better prioritize these needs.
